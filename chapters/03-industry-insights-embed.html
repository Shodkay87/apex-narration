What We Learned from Studying Roofing
Companies Across the Tri-State

Before building the Apex Revenue Recovery System, we studied the operational patterns that cause roofing companies to lose opportunities before the job is ever won.
Three findings consistently appeared.

Missed Calls Cost More
Than Most Owners Realize

Finding

Industry research shows many home-service businesses miss a meaningful percentage of inbound calls, and unanswered callers often move on instead of calling back.

Why It Matters

Every unanswered roofing call could represent an inspection, repair, or replacement opportunity that never reaches your team.

How Apex Helps

Ava answers immediately, captures the homeowner’s information, and alerts your team so opportunities don’t disappear after hours or during busy periods.

Invoca Home Services Call Analytics — 2024–2025

Speed Wins More
Roofing Jobs

Finding

Research shows businesses that respond quickly to new inquiries have a much greater chance of connecting with prospective customers than businesses that wait hours or days.

Why It Matters

Homeowners often contact multiple roofing companies. Delayed responses create opportunities for competitors.

How Apex Helps

Calls, web forms, and inquiries receive immediate attention so your team can respond while homeowners are still making decisions.

Harvard Business Review — “The Short Life of Online Sales Leads”

Storm Weeks Create
Operational Bottlenecks

Finding

Government data shows severe storm activity has increased across Pennsylvania and Maryland, creating sudden spikes in roofing demand.

Why It Matters

Storm weeks overwhelm office staff, increase missed calls, delay callbacks, and create scheduling bottlenecks.

How Apex Helps

The Revenue Recovery System continues answering calls, routing emergencies, updating your CRM, and notifying your team — even when demand surges.

NOAA National Centers for Environmental Information — Billion-Dollar Disaster Database

Good roofing companies don’t usually lose jobs because they can’t do the work.

They lose them because opportunities slip through the cracks before the work ever begins.
